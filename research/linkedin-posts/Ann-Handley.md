## Original Post

Hey so a few weeks ago I posted an SOS here for support because my new book deadline was a full month earlier than I expected.

Then I ghosted you. Initially, to ignore everything & rewrite rewrite <...pause to cry...> rewrite. Later, to try to calm my nervous system down and sleep and rest my left eyelid, which had started twitching somewhere between Chapter 1 and Chapter Done. 

Eyelid Twitch is a gentle, intermittent fluttering, like my eyelid is sending me distress signals in Morse code. "ABORT. We are sTreSS*d," Eyelid Twitch taps out. "R U ok?" 

I've been documenting Eyelid Twitch in a Gripe Journal: Daily grievance documentation for science, for history, for my future biographer writing how my story ended tragically after I was brought down by an undiagnosed brain bleed that presented as a gentle eyelid fluttering.

Anyway, the book. I never updated you. So (BIG NEWS!): I FINISHED THE BOOK. I HIT THE DEADLINE! (Please clap!)

Yet Eyelid Twitch remains. When I mention Eyelid Twitch (incessantly, if you must know), a lot of people pipe up.... "Yes! I once had an eyelid twitch, too!"

It's called eyelid myokymia, by the way. Myokymia comes from the Greek for “muscle” + “wave.” A waving muscle sounds beautiful and poetic until you realize it means your eyelid has stopped tapping out Morse code and that the “wave” is a small white flag of surrender from the *sTreSS*.

So now I'm bringing the question here, to you on LinkedIn: Can we bond over this?

Have you had Eyelid Twitch? 

Where did yours come from?

Stress...? Sleep deprivation...? Too much time staring glassy-eyed at Chapter 29 because wut r werds...? A Reply-All incident from 2016 your body never let go of...?

And most importantly, in the name of science and public service: how did you get it to GO AWAY?!
