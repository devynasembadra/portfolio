## Original Post

I’ve analysed thousands of email campaigns over the years.
And the thing that keeps showing up?
The biggest problem usually isn’t the copy.
Or the design.
Or even the strategy.

It’s that brands stop thinking like customers.
Emails become internal. Brand-centric. Broadcasty. Disconnected from what real people actually care about.

The best-performing campaigns tend to do something much simpler, and much harder: They meet people where they already are. Clear offer. Clear value.
Clear next step.

Not hacks.
Not tricks.
Just better thinking.
